About

This blog is the current implementation of a long lineage of blogs, dating back to the mid-90s and the time when Geocities and Angelfire reigned supreme.

I am currently a developer/student at Hacker School in New York City! IT IS FANTASTIC.

»»»«««

In 2006 I was diagnosed with Acute Myelogenous Leukemia. I was 20 years old and a junior in undergrad. It was a terrifying, humbling, and life-changing experience. It's left an indelible mark on how I view health, God, work, and life.

In January 2012 I launched Cheekswab.org, a website/movement dedicated to spreading the word about bone marrow donation to ethnic minority groups. Read more about Cheekswab here.

In August 2012 I reached my five year post-diagnosis mark and was declared cured by my doctors. It was one of the greatest days of my life.

»»»«««

This blog is built on Jekyll, which is a static-site generator written in Ruby. Read more about that here.

I bought a Lumix GF-1 on impulse off of craigslist about a year ago. I love its size and capabilities, and it's forgiving enough to make a photography hack like myself seem semi-competent. The majority of the photos on this site will be taken with that camera, like the one you see above.

This blog isn't limited to any topic in particular. I do enjoy writing and would like to complete more long-form pieces, but only time will tell how successfully I do that. I'd like to experiment specifically with typography, layout and photos.

I'm on Twitter, LinkedIn and github. For everything else, please visit http://www.danielna.com.
